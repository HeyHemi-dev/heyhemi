# Product

# Market

<aside>
1️⃣ Problem

1. People want inspiration for planning their wedding.
2. Pinterest helps create a vision but doesn’t provide an easy way to buy or source the things pinned.
</aside>

<aside>
4️⃣ Solution

- Vendor-created pins with supplier tagging.
- Search and filtering options.
- Detailed vendor profiles.
- User-created boards for organization.
- Private user pins for collaboration.
</aside>

<aside>
8️⃣ Key Metrics

- Monthly Active Users (MAU).
- Pins saved by users.
- Pins created by vendors.
- Average vendors tagged per pin.
- User clickthrough to vendor profiles.
- User clicks to vendor contact information.
</aside>

<aside>
3️⃣ Unique Value Prop

A platform that allows users to pin their dream wedding ideas while connecting directly with the vendors who can bring those dreams to life. Featuring vendor-created pins tagged with all relevant suppliers.

### High-Level Concept

Pinterest mets Amazon (for Weddings) —aspiration and purchase combined.

</aside>

<aside>
9️⃣ Unfair Advantage

- Established vendor relationships through 13 years of experience in the New Zealand wedding industry.
- Local focus and expertise, difficult for global platforms like Pinterest to replicate effectively.
- Potential for network effects as the platform grows.
</aside>

<aside>
5️⃣ Channels

- Social media marketing (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok).
- Partnerships with wedding vendors.
- Wedding expos and events.
- SEO with a strong URL strategy and schema.
</aside>

<aside>
2️⃣ Customer Segments

Primary: Wedding couples, primarily women aged 20-35, in New Zealand.

Secondary: Wedding planners.

</aside>

<aside>
7️⃣ Cost Structure

- Development costs.
- Server costs, especially for image storage.
- Marketing costs.
</aside>

<aside>
6️⃣ Revenue Streams

- Promoted pins by vendors.
- Charging vendors for regional visibility expansion.
- Premium vendor profile features.
</aside>
